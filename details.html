K Index
Also known as "Soaring Index". The warmer the air, the more this index matters. The primary purpose is to measure stability as a function of temperature and humidity.
 
-10 to 5 Dry, moderate thermals; 1/8 cumulus
5 to 15 OK soaring, mostly dry
15 to 20 Good soaring, showers possible
20 to 30 Excellent soaring, showers more likely
30+ >60% chance of thunderstorms
CAPE
Acronymn for "Convective Available Potential Energy". A measurement of the potential energy a thunderstorm could have, if one develops. Increasing numbers indicate higher instability and convective energy.
 
0 to 300 Mild to moderate energy
300 to 600 Moderate to high energy
600 to 900+ High energy, trending to severe
Lifted Index (LI)
A measurement of general stability (positive values) or instability (negative values). As values trend stronger negative, lift becomes more abundant and energetic, but risk of thunderstorms also increases.
0+ Stable, limited lift
-1 to 0 Sweet spot
-2 to -1 Thunderstorms possible
-3 to -2 Unstable, thunderstorms likely
